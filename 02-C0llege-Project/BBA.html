PROFILE

Bachelor of Business Management was established in the year 1992. The three year program is offered with an intake of 90 students divided into two sections. This program is affiliated to Andhra University.
The curricular track prescribed by the University provides the core commerce and management subjects designed for the purpose of imparting knowledge in all functional areas of the management. In order to integrate core business fundamentals with the needed specialized skills, the program offers add-on courses and activities that support dynamic learning to prepare the students for constantly changing business environment. A team of specialist soft-skills trainers hone the soft-skills and communication of the students through SDC sessions.

Objectives

The department works with the following objectives:

• Student should utilize knowledge of management theory to achieve business objectives.
• Student should have the understanding of how organizations are led and managed
• Student shout develop the ability to understand and utilize business data in decision making.
• Student should acquire critical and analytical skills to create innovative solutions to business challenges.
• Student should understand the ethical obligations pertaining to business and society with empathy.
• Exhibit appreciation of multiple perspectives and cultural differences within a team.

Faculty

The department is run by experienced faculty with an understanding of student needs to the core. Each faculty is assigned with a group of students. Faculty pays personal attention to each one of the assigned students to make them successful in an increasingly competitive world. The faculty also takes active interest in research and publications in leading journals, participate and presenting research papers in national seminars to keep themselves abreast with the management theory and practice

Pedagogy

Subject Curriculum and Teaching Aids:

Curricular subjects are dealt with for four sessions each per week during a semester. Besides conventional blackboard and regular classroom environment, teaching is carried out with the use of LCD projectors, audio visual equipment in seminar halls designated for the course. A well equipped computer lab with internet facility is available for the students to make use of information technology for learning besides acquainting with on-line examination system on subject areas to fine tune the fundamentals necessary for their placements. However, the subjects and subject delivery mechanism is periodically revised to suit the market. Value Add - on Programme

Two add-on courses are given to the students one each in 1st and 2nd year. Four sessions in a week are demarcated to deal with the add-on program. The aim of the department is to broaden the academic scope by including specific subjects as add-on programs to fill in the gap between the acquired and the required market related knowledge. The Add on programmes are :
• Six Sigma
• Value Proportion in Business Models,
• Dealing with difficult people
• Emotional Intelligence
• Ethics in Corporate house
• Financial Derivatives etc.

Business Line and Harvard Business Review Programme

Employment is not the only objective of education, building life skills and personality development are also important, keeping this in mind department provided six session per week for the Business Line classes to provide business news to the students and monthly four sessions for Harvard Business Review (HBR) to the students, during this session students will present the articles from the HBR under the guidance of dean and faculty, which helps the students to enrich their knowledge and presentation skills and makes them to stand in Global Competition.

Soft Skill Development

Four sessions per week are set apart to provide the students with Skill Development where classes are held to hone the soft skills, better communication, to improve team work and Kinesics. Here the student gets an opportunity to learn about oneself, apply that knowledge, participate in leadership tasks, Group discussions etc that will transform them into thorough management professionals

Employable Skills

The employable skills programmes we offer are: Communication skills, Soft skills, Arithmetic Skills and Reasoning Skills. Tally Computerised Accounting programme offered to B.Com students

Orator Programme

College is providing orator program to the students to develop presentation and leadership skills in the students to face challenges more confidently

Extracurricular Activities

College encourages students to participate in extracurricular activities grouped into clubs besides sports and games, NCC and NSS activities aiming to make the students well-rounded personalities

Placement

The Career Guidance and Placement Cell (CGPC) runs concurrent to the department to help the students connect with industry networks of business and professionals. CGPC also conducts periodical placement drives on campus and assists each one to attend off-campus drives conducted by the university and other colleges. The department, CGPC and SDC work in tandem on various activities to train and prepare students to face interviews successfully.
Every year more than 20 reputed companies have been visiting the campus and students stand fair chances to get a job in reputed companies at the end of their studies in the college
